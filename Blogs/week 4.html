Passionate Design: The Design Process

10 March 2025

Design Vision

My design process is mostly smooth. I noticed how I changed a lot of things. I want my website to have a modern, Pinterest-like aesthetic with gradient pastel colors and minimalistic elements. In essence, it should look like this:

Ideal homepage wireframe – simple nav, playful title, and gradient background.

Above is my ideal homepage; it has a navigation bar with a simple font, a playful title (that won't be the real title), and a little introductory text. Next to it are little profiles you can click on that will act like the navigation bar. The gradient background color is subject to change slightly.

Portfolio Page

This is what my portfolio page will look like. Very minimalistic.

Portfolio layout – clean and simple display of work.

Blog Page

My blog page will have grids as shown below.

Blog page grid – organized and engaging for reading.

About Page

My about page will provide adequate information about me. On either side of the screen, there will be links to the previous pages in the website and/or any other links.

About page design – informative and connected with other pages.

Contact Page

The last page, which is the contacts page, will have everything the viewer will need to get a hold of me and will also look something like the about page.

Contact page – inviting collaboration and connection.

Reflections

For this design wireframe I used Canva to create it.

Overall, my progress for Website One isn't all bad, although I know I could still do more. I am struggling with filtering the kind of information that is good practice versus brain rot. Even with the information overload, I am proud of the basics I have gotten down so far. Moving forward, I want to make it look better and run smoothly.
